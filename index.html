Introduction:

I am a post-doctoral research at Academia Sinica, Taiwan. I defended my PhD thesis at University of Gothenburg, Sweden under the supervision of Prof. Aarne Ranta in June 2013. My main research interests are in the area of natural language modeling, computational resources development, and more recently in the area of computational semantics. During my PhD, I worked in the Grammatical Framework group at University of Gothenburg, Sweden. As a part of my PhD studies, I developed computational resources (both syntactical and lexical) for six Indo-Iranian languages including Hindi, Urdu, Persian, Punjabi, Nepali, and Sindhi. These resources are in the form of software libraries and encode different syntactical and lexical aspects of these natural languages. Historically, such resources have proved to be very useful in building domain specific application grammars both commercial and academic. Further, we are exploring the possibilities of using these resources to build a multilingual arbitrary text translator. Currently, I am working on semantic role labeling and its applications.
