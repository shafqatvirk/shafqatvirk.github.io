Introduction:

I defended my PhD thesis at University of Gothenburg, Sweden under the supervision of Prof. Aarne Ranta in June 2013. Currently, I am a post-doctoral research fellow at IRIT Lab, Paul Sabatier University, Toulouse, France.
Broadly speaking, my main research interests are in the areas of natural language processing (NLP), particularly in grammar engineering and computational linguistic resources development, and machine learning. During PhD, I worked with the Grammatical Framework (GF) group at University of Gothenburg, and developed computational resources (both syntactical and lexical) for six Indo-Iranian languages including Hindi, Urdu, Persian, Punjabi, Nepali, and Sindhi. These resources are in the form of software libraries and encode different syntactical and lexical aspects of these natural languages. Historically, such resources have proved to be very useful in building domain specific application grammars both commercial and academic. See GF Homepage for more details.
I am also interesed in semantics, and more recently, I have been involved in semantic parsing. I worked on shallow semantic parsing during a one-year postdoctral position at Academia Sinica, Taiwan from August 2013 to September 2014. We developed shallow semantic parsing systems for English and Chineese, and a concept-parser utilizing the semantic parsers (see Publications and Systems section for more details). At the moment, I am working on a frame-based sematnic role labeling system for French. The main idea is to exploit inter-frame relations and use linear integer programming for better results.
